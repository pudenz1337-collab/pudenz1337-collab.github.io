How to Measure
📋 Tips for consistency: Measure at the same time of day (morning is best). Relax muscles — don't flex. Use a fabric tape measure, not metal. Pull snug but not tight. Same day each month.
📍
Waist Most important
Find your natural waist — the narrowest point of your torso, typically 1 inch above your belly button. Stand relaxed, exhale naturally, measure. This is your best proxy for visceral fat reduction — more reliable than scale weight.
📍
Hips Most important
Stand with feet together. Measure at the widest point across your glutes and hips — usually about 8 inches below your natural waist. Keep the tape parallel to the floor all the way around.
📐
Chest
Arms relaxed at sides. Measure across the fullest part of your chest, keeping the tape under your armpits and across your shoulder blades in the back. Breathe normally, don't inhale to expand.
💪
Right Arm (bicep)
Flex your right arm at 90 degrees, make a fist. Measure around the peak of the bicep at the widest point. Always measure right side — your dominant side will naturally be slightly larger, but consistency is what matters for tracking change.
🦵
Right Thigh
Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, weight evenly distributed. Find the midpoint between your hip crease (where your thigh meets your torso when you bend forward) and the top of your knee. Measure around the thigh at that point.
🦵
Right Calf
Stand on flat ground, weight evenly distributed. Measure at the widest point of your calf — usually about one-third of the way down from your knee. Don't flex or stand on your toes.
